How to Read the Scores

The FTSE 100 ESG Exposure Index uses a contrarian framework. Higher values reflect greater ESG-related commitments, programmes, incentives, and reporting intensity.

Score Interpretation

Lower scores rank more favorably on the Index. A company with a score of 0.15 ranks more favorably than a company with a score of 0.65.

Best
0.00 – 0.25

Minimal ESG intensity; fewer commitments and lower reporting.

Moderate
0.25 – 0.50

Low to moderate ESG intensity; selective programs and moderate reporting.

Poor
0.50 – 0.75

High ESG intensity; established commitments and regular reporting.

Wasteful
0.75 – 1.00

Maximum ESG intensity; extensive programs prioritizing ESG over shareholders.

The Three Dimensions

Environment (E): Climate targets, investment in transition, and climate reporting.

Social (S): DEI targets, workplace programs, and social incentives.

Governance (G): Board diversity, executive compensation structures, and governance practices.
